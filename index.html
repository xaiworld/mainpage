About Me

Hello! I'm the person behind xai.world. I'm a software engineer working for a Swiss company, living in the beautiful town of Manzanares el Real, nestled at the foot of La Pedriza in the Sierra de Guadarrama.

When I'm not writing code, you'll find me deep into a boardgame, running mountain trails, playing music, reading sci-fi, or peering through a microscope. This site is my home on the internet — a hub connecting all the different worlds I inhabit.

Q&A

Things people ask me.

How did you get into the Brass boardgame?

I first played Brass: Lancashire around 2011 and was hooked instantly. Over 600 games later, I'm still obsessed with its economic strategy and canal/rail mechanics. I even built an online implementation at brass.xai.world.

What do you grow in your garden?

A mix of vegetables, herbs, and flowers. I'm especially into tomatoes, basil, and pollinator-friendly plants. The garden changes every season — check gardening.xai.world for updates.

Galician bagpipes — really?

Really! The Galician gaita is an incredible instrument with deep Celtic roots. I also play Irish whistle and baritone ukulele — quite the eclectic mix!

What tech stack do you use for these sites?

This main page is plain HTML and CSS hosted on Render. The subdomains vary — some are static, some use lightweight frameworks. I believe in keeping things simple until complexity is actually needed.

Can I reach out to you?

Sure! The best way is by opening an issue on the GitHub repo.
